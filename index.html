Challenge Alignment & Solution

EcoTrace aligns the Carbon Footprint Awareness problem statement with a zero-dependency, secure-by-default, and fully accessible engineering implementation.

The Problem

Understanding the Challenge

Existing carbon calculators and eco-platforms face several critical flaws that undermine their adoption, security, and usability:

  • ✗
    Data Privacy & Tracking Risks: Many tools require accounts, transmit sensitive habits to third parties, or use external tracking scripts.
  • ✗
    Access & Inclusivity Barriers: Heavy visual dashboards often completely lock out users relying on screen readers or keyboard-only navigation.
  • ✗
    Supply-Chain Vulnerabilities: Dashboards rely heavily on NPM libraries and external scripts, making them vulnerable to malicious injections or domain hijackings.
  • ✗
    Performance Bloat: Massive charting frameworks (like ChartJS/D3) slow down page loads, consume user bandwidth, and limit offline usability.
Our Solution

How EcoTrace Stands Out

EcoTrace addresses every aspect of the problem statement using raw, high-performance web standards to deliver a superior, secure, and accessible platform:

  • ✓
    Zero-Dependency Core: Built entirely using native HTML5, CSS3, and ES6+ JS. Sub-millisecond performance with zero susceptibility to NPM supply-chain exploits.
  • ✓
    Hardened Node.js Server: Custom server equipped with a strict path-traversal barrier and headers preventing clickjacking, MIME-sniffing, and cross-site scripting (XSS).
  • ✓
    100% WCAG 2.1 AA Compliant: Seamless keyboard tab navigation, custom focus states, explicit input labeling, and dynamic screen reader live announcements.
  • ✓
    Offline-Capable Climate AI: Features "EcoBot"—a local assistant analyzing live carbon variables dynamically without requiring any network requests.
  • ✓
    Native SVG Charting: Interactive segment arcs are drawn directly using native SVG calculations, eliminating library bloat.

    Understanding Your Impact

    Every activity creates carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases. Estimating your footprint makes it clear where carbon accumulates in your life.

    • Transport: Burning gasoline or diesel is highly carbon-intensive. Driving less or carpooling creates the most immediate impact.
    • Home Energy: Space heating, air conditioning, and appliances burn fossil fuels upstream. LED bulbs, insulating windows, and reducing HVAC use save money and CO₂.
    • Waste: Landfills produce methane—a greenhouse gas 28x more potent than carbon dioxide. Composting organic material reduces this significantly.
